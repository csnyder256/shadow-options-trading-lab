## Proven critical findings

1. **The 06-30 "31.57% drawdown" halt was a $92.27 withdrawal.** `rh_mcp.log` shows `total_value`
   292.27 → exactly 200.00 at 11:37:29 ET and it stays there (the account holds $199.99 today - 
   verified against the broker). `update_drawdown` nets no external cash flows, so a withdrawal on a
   flat, tradeless account read as (292.27−200)/292.27 = 31.57% and tripped DISABLED_REVIEW.
   Symmetrically, a **deposit** would have silently **masked** a real drawdown. **FIXED**: cash-flow
   reconciliation (flat-book + no-fills only, so real losses can never be misclassified), `equity_flow`
   journal event.
2. **DISABLED_REVIEW ("only a human may clear") evaporated in 12 minutes, unlogged.** The escalation
   only persisted at cycle END, and the 11:49 clear was a hand-edit of `risk_state.runtime.json`
   (per the runbook) with no journal trace. **FIXED**: synchronous persist at the transition +
   `state_overridden` journal event whenever a de-escalation this process didn't perform appears.
3. **The kill switch is silent.** No alert of any kind fires on halt/DISABLED_REVIEW; 06-30 and the
   07-01 crash windows (27 + 33 min) buzzed nobody. **FIXED**: `alert_watch` now pushes on `halt`
   events, on a persisted halted state during market hours, and on the app log going silent >10 min;
   its journal ordering is now by timestamp (cycle ids reset on relaunch and broke the stall detector).
4. **The launcher killed the protectors.** On app crash (twice in 2 days) the `finally` block
   force-killed the Guardian (sole protector of fractional lots) and alert_watch - the exact
   "unprotected position + silence" failure the design exists to prevent. On Guardian death it killed
   the app **before** the app's own 90s dead-man flatten could act. **FIXED**: app crash → up to 3
   restarts with fresh log files (old redirect truncated the crash traceback); Guardian death → one
   restart, dead-man flatten as backstop; protectors + alerter run to the close no matter what.
5. **Swing brackets + unconditional same-day flatten = structurally EV-negative.** Stop = 3.5×ATR(14,
   daily) ≈ 5.6 daily σ; target ≈ 11 σ; entry mid-day; forced flatten at 15:50. First-passage math:
   P(stop) ≈ 2×10⁻⁴, P(target) ≈ 10⁻¹³ for a 9:40 entry at σ=1.5%/day - ≥99.98% of trades exit as
   noise minus market-in/market-out costs. The FRT −$0.01 was the theorem's first data point, and the
   previous session's "fix" (30-min entry blackout) amputated the 13:00–16:00 window where **all**
   live consensus passes have occurred, without touching the cause. **FIXED** (Design C, hybrid):
   - Guardian `eod_flat_mode: losers_only` - at EOD only lots below entry (−0.10% buffer) or with an
     unreadable quote are flushed; winners/flat lots **ride overnight** with levels persisted and
     re-enforced at the next open; a 5-session time-stop bounds total hold; stop/target breaches
     inside the window still exit normally.
   - `market_close_blackout_min` 30 → 5 (fill + Guardian-ack margin only), and it's pre-checked
     BEFORE the LLM cascade so guaranteed-reject candidates stop burning ~55s of model time.
   - Blackouts are now evaluated on an elapsed-adjusted clock at decision time (the FRT order hit RH
     ~3 min after its cycle's clock was stamped - order latency was actually 160 ms; the lag was the
     cascade runtime).
6. **Every server-side exit was journaled `reason: "stop"`** (hardcoded in the adapter's
   position-delta detector) and `guardian_exits.jsonl` - which carries the true reason - was drained
   by NOBODY. The FRT EOD scratch therefore registered `consecutive_losses=1` **and armed the 60-min
   revenge cooldown**: forced churn was being counted as trading failure. **FIXED**: the orchestrator
   now looks up the Guardian's true exit reason for synthetic lots; wins count on any positive exit,
   losses only on stop-like exits or real money lost (>0.25%); scratches mutate nothing. Runtime
   state was cleaned (streak 0, cooldown null).
7. **Busy-spin: `did_work` counted pool depth.** After the daily budget was spent (or a halt), the
   loop ran at ~1–2s/cycle - ~26 `run_scan`/min, 7,224 `get_equity_positions` on 6/29 - burning the
   shared RH rate budget exactly while the Guardian was protecting a live lot, and corrupting every
   cycle-denominated timeout. **FIXED**: `did_work` now means the cascade/orders/exits actually ran
   and nothing was skipped; skipped cycles always sleep the idle cadence.

## High-severity funnel defects (all fixed today)

- **`vol_zscore` on today's PARTIAL daily bar**: reads −1..−2 all morning on names the scan REQUIRED
  RVOL ≥1.3–1.8 to surface; constitution rule 9 then mandates the veto (33/55 live vetoes; the 90%
  live vs 60% test gap - the test harness fed complete bars). FIXED: the last bar's volume is
  projected to a full-day estimate by elapsed session fraction (feed-lag aware, floored) before
  z-scoring, and the scan's consolidated `relative_volume_day` is passed through to the LLM features.
- **Liquidity gate on IEX-only volume** (understates the consolidated tape ~14×; called MSM/$240M-a-day
  "thin"): FIXED - the floor now uses the scan row's consolidated `last×volume/RVOL` when available.

  FIXED - same-day rejections escalate 30m → 2h → 8h per (symbol, setup).
- **One-cycle universe-admission window** (a >$100 approval died unless its own scan re-polled that
  cycle; FRT threaded a 1-in-3 window): FIXED - admission is sticky for the candidate's pool lifetime.
- **Consensus operating point**: `risk_weight` 0.70 was the docs worked-example number, never
  calibrated; on the project's own forensic ledger w=0.5 gives 35 trades/+12.29R vs 21/+11.70R.
  RETUNED to 0.50 (floor 57 and the hard risk≥60 veto unchanged). Counterfactual on the last 3 live

- **Frequency caps vs the mission**: launcher hardcoded `--max-trades 1` ("day-1 caution", 6/6

  3 concurrent, launcher default defers to config; the cooldown check is now actually wired into the
  risk engine (it was written but never read) and only real stop-outs arm it.
- **llama-swap `ttl: 300`** guaranteed cold ~20GB reloads between drip-fed evaluations (GLM
