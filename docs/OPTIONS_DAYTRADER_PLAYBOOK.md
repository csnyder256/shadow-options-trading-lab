## 6. POST-TRADE ANALYTICS (nightly compute lab)

### 6.1 Spread-fraction breakeven f* - the kill decision made continuous
- **Trigger/Circumstance:** Every lane's N=25 verdict.
- **What a seasoned trader does:** Knows WORST (full half-spread each way) is a floor real executors demonstrably beat: Muravyev–Pearson (RFS 2020, Table 5) - quoted half-spread 8.6%, conventional effective 6.8% (~0.79×), timing-adjusted all-trader 5.0% (~58%), execution-timers 2.5% (29.6%). A lane failing only at full spread may be execution-bound, not signal-dead.
- **What WE do:** GRADING-RULE: nightly, recompute each lane's PnL as a function of assumed half-spread fraction f ∈ {0, 0.1, …, 1.0} on stored NBBO; report f* where PnL crosses zero. Bands: f* ≥1.0 = robust (WORST-positive, deployable); 0.35 ≤ f* <1.0 = **EXECUTION-BOUND, not signal-dead** (viable only with timed executions; the prescribed research fix is entry/exit timing relative to a fair-value estimate within the quote cycle - mid-limit fills are OUR modeling proxy, not the paper's finding); f* <0.35 = dead. Also report the pre-registered "spread share" = (BASE−WORST)/|BASE| per lane; WORST<0<BASE with spread share >50% maps to the EXECUTION-BOUND verdict class. Label the 0.35 ledger "average execution-timer (M&P algo ≈0.296×)"; note 0.584 is the timing-adjusted ALL-trader average - a lane failing at 0.584 fails harder at a true naive-taker calibration (~0.79×).
- **Status:** GRADING-RULE (CONFIRMED benchmarks; MODIFIED attribution - quoted-vs-effective baseline and mechanism labels corrected).
- **Sources:** https://academic.oup.com/rfs/article-abstract/33/11/4973/5732665 · https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2580548

### 6.2 Gross-vs-WORST decomposition by exit-time bucket
- **Trigger/Circumstance:** Every lane verdict; every exit cohort by time of day.
- **What a seasoned trader does:** Separates friction from signal: the spread-free comparator is mid-to-mid GROSS (by construction worst_net + spread_paid = gross) - NOT the BASE-vs-WORST gap (BASE captures only 0.65× the tax). Knows the timing evidence per window: 15:55 and 16:00–16:10 exits sit in the documented index-option deterioration zone (Cao–Jacobs–Ke: %-spreads elevated before the close, dollar spreads rising into 16:00, sizes troughing - though spreads shrink again 16:00–16:15); the 15:00–15:30 0DTE window is taxed via the RELATIVE channel (half-spread/premium explodes as premium collapses); single-name equity options show NO close spike (CCJ 1995). Cost benchmarks: 0DTE retail effective spreads 6.0% calls / 5.0% puts vs 12.5%/9.6% non-retail (Beckmeyer–Branger–Gayda, Dec-2023 version); retail quoted ≈2× retail effective (price improvement "cuts effective spreads in half" even near expiration); quoted spreads roughly DOUBLE under 1 hour to expiry (retail QS 9.6%→19.0%).
- **What WE do:** GRADING-RULE: infrastructure already persists the pieces (`atlas/options/shadow.py` `build_exit_record`: NBBO at both fills + `decomposition.spread_paid_usd`; scorecard: `net_worst_mean`, `net_base_mean`, `gross_sum`, `cost_share_of_losses`) - add only two derived fields: half_spread/premium at entry and at exit (per-leg split). Report every N=25 verdict twice: raw WORST and mid-to-mid gross, bucketed by exit time (pre-15:00 / 15:00–15:45 / post-15:45). **Kill a lane only if BOTH are negative**; raw-WORST-fails-but-gross-passes → the registered fix is exit-timing, not signal death. (This matters now: the current PASS bar at `grade_options_shadow.py:123` requires worst-mean>0 AND gross>0 - exactly the failure mode this rule guards.) Document that WORST overstates realistic retail cost by roughly 2× per the quoted-vs-effective evidence.
- **Status:** GRADING-RULE (MODIFIED - decomposition identity and Beckmeyer figures corrected; the "$0.50→$3.00" widening figure is deleted as unsourced).

### 6.3 Post-fill markout curves - the prop-desk diagnostic retail never runs
- **Trigger/Circumstance:** Every entry and exit fill, all three ledgers, computed offline from stored mark paths.
- **What a seasoned trader does:** Measures adverse selection with markouts: signed mid-to-mid (and fill-to-mid) change at fixed horizons after every fill, aggregated per strategy. Entry markouts persistently negative at +5 min = the signal is late (buying after the move - the exact failure the equity replay found in day0 momentum); exit markouts persistently positive = the exit stack is selling winners too early.
- **What WE do:** Nightly module: markout_k = side × (mid_{t+k} − fill_price) for k ∈ {+1, +5, +15, +30} min, in option-premium terms AND a delta-adjusted underlying variant (entry delta strips market drift); aggregated per lane and per triggering exit rule. Pre-registered signals: median entry markout_5 < −0.25× half-spread = "adversely selected" flag in the scorecard (lane kill signal at N=25 if persistent beyond spread); persistently positive exit markouts = evidence a specific rule (e.g., the +100% take) truncates winners. Report-only until N=25.
- **Status:** COVARIATE → GRADING input (PASSIVE - high confidence; standard method).

### 6.4 MFE/MAE, exit efficiency, and exit-lag - ranking the 26 exit rules
- **Trigger/Circumstance:** Every closed trade; the 26-rule first-match exit engine has never been ranked internally.
- **What a seasoned trader does:** Grades exits independently of entries via excursion analysis (Sweeney, *Campaign Trading*, 1996): exit_efficiency = realized P&L / MFE (~0.5 solid, ~0.75 excellent by practitioner convention); attributes MFE truncation to the specific rule that fired; measures what recognition lag costs.
- **What WE do:** Nightly per trade on the BASE-ledger path: MFE, MAE, time-of-MFE, exit_efficiency (when MFE>0), and exit_lag_cost = mark value at first rule-breach timestamp − value at the actual graded exit mark; broken out by triggering exit rule and lane. The −50% stop's MAE distribution tells us whether it sits inside noise; the +100%-take vs 25%-off-peak-trail fork is directly scoreable as captured MFE. Feed the replay engine the pre-registered variant grid: stop −40/−50/−60%, take +80/+100%, off-peak trail 20/25/30% - scored by paired exit_efficiency + markout. Pre-registered follow-on: if median 0DTE exit_lag_cost >5% of premium, the 2-min afternoon cadence becomes 1-min (a data change → go-live-first class A).
- **Status:** COVARIATE → GRADING input (PASSIVE - high/medium confidence; method standard, thresholds convention).

### 6.5 Build our own intraday spread curve (replace literature priors in ~30 sessions)
- **Trigger/Circumstance:** Every entry, exit, and stored mark, from day 1.
- **What a seasoned trader does:** Knows MMs hold quoted spreads roughly flat but cut DEPTH near the close (usask CBOE study) - harmless at 1-lot (firm quote covers it) but worth verifying on own data rather than extending the midday curve to 15:30–15:55; also that the 16:00–16:15 window has NO rigorous published spread measurement (our logs would be the first clean one).
- **What WE do:** Log quoted spread (ticks and % of mid) at entry, exit, and every mark, bucketed 09:35–10 / 10–11 / 11–14 / 14–15:30 / 15:30–15:55 / 15:55–16:15, per lane and per symbol; plus the daily 15:55/16:00/16:05/16:10 half-spread ladder for any position open at the close (feeds 4.6's retirement trigger). After ~30 sessions this in-house curve replaces every literature prior in this document's spread entries; any change to `last30`'s 15:55 exit or the post-16:00 window is gated on it.
