## 2. PUBLISHED UNIVERSE & OUR MAPPING

**Published universe:** 21 liquid U.S. futures markets on Chicago and New York exchanges - bonds/notes/Eurodollar/T-bill, coffee, cocoa, sugar, cotton, five currencies, S&P 500 stock index futures, gold, silver, copper, crude, heating oil, unleaded gas. "In general, the Turtles traded all liquid U.S. markets except the grains and the meats." Selection criterion: "The primary criterion used to determine the futures that could be traded by the Turtles was the liquidity of the underlying markets." (Ch. 2, pp. 10–11.)

**Our mapping:** SPY / QQQ / IWM plus the liquid mega-cap tier AAPL / NVDA / MSFT / TSLA / AMD / META; signal computed on the underlying's daily bars, position expressed as a 1-lot debit vertical in the trend direction (long breakout → bull call spread; short breakout → bear put spread).

**Why the mapping is defensible:** the S&P 500 index was itself in the published portfolio ("S&P 500 Stock Index," CME list, p. 11), and the published market-selection rule is liquidity, not asset class - our nine names are among the most liquid option underlyings in existence. The signal layer (channel breakout, N-based stop, trailing channel exit) transfers unchanged.

**ADAPTATIONS - DOCUMENTED LOUDLY:**
1. **Futures → options.** Options-only platform: no futures, no stock legs. The linear futures payoff becomes a **capped** debit-vertical payoff. This is the single biggest distortion: the published edge is explicitly a fat-right-tail edge ("most of the profits in a given year might come from only two or three large winning trades," p. 20; "Most breakouts do not result in trends. This means that most of the trades that the Turtles made resulted in losses," p. 25). A vertical truncates exactly the tail that pays for the losers. Recorded as failure mode F1 (Section 7).
2. **21 loosely-correlated markets → 9 highly-correlated equity underlyings.** The published diversification benefit ("Proper diversification requires making similar, if not identical bets on many different instruments," p. 8) is mostly lost; signals will cluster.
3. **No pyramiding.** Published rule adds up to 4 Units at ½N intervals; our 1-lot account-blind shadow takes exactly one spread per signal. Constants recorded but NOT ARMED (Section 8 rows 13–14).
4. **Defined-risk structure replaces the 2N stop as the loss backstop**, but we still implement the 2N underlying-level stop as an active exit (Section 4, X2) because it is also load-bearing for the entry filter's winner/loser definition.

## 3. EXACT ENTRY RULES

We implement **System 1 with its 55-day Failsafe** (the shorter-term published system). System 2 constants are recorded (Section 8) but not armed - same choice as the secondary source's test ("system 2, not tested" - Garner 2010).

- **E-TRIG (breakout definition):** "A breakout is defined as the price exceeding the high or low of a particular number of days. Thus a 20-day breakout would be defined as exceeding the high or low of the preceding 20 days." (Ch. 4, p. 18.)
- **E1 (System 1 entry, both directions):** "Turtles entered positions when the price exceeded by a single tick the high or low of the preceding 20 days. If the price exceeded the 20-day high, then the Turtles would buy one Unit to initiate a long position … If the price dropped one tick below the low of the last 20-days, the Turtles would sell one Unit to initiate a short position." (Ch. 4, p. 19.) → long breakout buys a bull call vertical; short breakout buys a bear put vertical (structure ADAPTED).
- **E2 (last-breakout-winner filter, System 1 only):** "System 1 breakout entry signals would be ignored if the last breakout would have resulted in a winning trade. NOTE: … the last breakout was considered the last breakout in the particular commodity irrespective of whether or not that particular breakout was actually taken, or was skipped because of this rule. This breakout would be considered a losing breakout if the price subsequent to the date of the breakout moved 2N against the position before a profitable 10-day exit occurred. The direction of the last breakout was irrelevant to this rule." (Ch. 4, p. 19.)
- **E3 (Failsafe entry):** "However, in the event that a System 1 entry breakout was skipped because the previous trade had been a winner, an entry would be made at the 55-day breakout to avoid missing major moves. This 55-day breakout was considered the Failsafe Breakout point." (Ch. 4, p. 19.)
- **E4 (entry timing - intraday, not close):** "Turtles always traded at the breakout when it was exceeded during the day, and did not wait until the daily close or the open of the following day. In the case of opening gaps, the Turtles would enter positions on the open if a market opened through the price of the breakout." (Ch. 4, p. 18.) → we monitor the underlying intraday against the prior-20-day channel and fire same-day.
- **E5 (order tactics):** "We were also told that, in general, it was better to place limit orders instead of market orders." (Ch. 7, p. 27.) → spread order at mid, platform marketable-limit convention.
- **Volatility unit N (used by E2 and the exits):** "N is simply the 20-day exponential moving average of the True Range, which is now more commonly known as the ATR." Formula: True Range = Maximum(H − L, H − PDC, PDC − L); N = (19 × PDN + TR) / 20; "you must start with a 20-day simple average of the True Range for the initial calculation." (Ch. 3, pp. 12–13.)
- **Strike selection:** NO PUBLISHED VALUE - no options in source. ADAPTED: long leg at the listed strike nearest spot (ATM); short leg at the strike nearest (entry ± 2N) in the trend direction, minimum one strike apart. Rationale: 2N is the only price-distance constant published by the source (stop distance and losing-breakout test), so the cap sits where the published system would have conceded the trade thesis was wrong in the opposite direction. Delta target: UNKNOWN in source.
- **DTE selection:** NO PUBLISHED VALUE. ADAPTED: nearest monthly expiration with 45–75 DTE. Rationale anchored to the secondary source's measured trade durations: "43 calendar days for winning trades and 13 days for losing trades" (Garner 2010, Table 4) - the expiry must outlive a typical winner plus the trailing exit lag.
- **Regime / IV gate:** NONE PUBLISHED. The source contains no volatility-regime, IV, or event filter of any kind; the only filter is E2. We add none (honest absence, recorded in Section 8 row 22).
- **Event timing (T-n for earnings/FOMC):** NONE PUBLISHED - the Turtles entered on price alone. No event gate armed; earnings proximity is logged observe-only (PLATFORM-POLICY).

## 4. EXACT EXIT RULES - OVERRIDE ALL PLATFORM EXIT DOCTRINE FOR THIS STRATEGY

- **X1 (trailing channel exit - the primary exit):** "The System 1 exit was a 10 day low for long positions and a 10 day high for short positions. All the Units in the position would be exited if the price went against the position for a 10 day breakout." (Ch. 6, p. 26.) Intraday, not on close: "the Turtles did not typically place exit stop orders, but instead watched the price during the day, and started to phone in exit orders as soon as the price traded through the exit breakout price." (p. 26.) → close the entire spread the day the underlying trades through the 10-day opposite extreme.
- **X2 (stop):** "Turtle stops were set at 2 N below the entry for long positions, and 2 N above the entry for short positions." "These stops were non-negotiable exits. If a particular commodity traded at the stop price, then the position was exited; each time, every time, without fail." (Ch. 5, pp. 21–22.) → underlying-level stop at entry-price ∓ 2N (N frozen at entry); trading through it closes the spread same day. Note the spread's max loss (debit) already bounds the dollar risk; X2 is kept anyway because it is the published behavior and it stops theta bleed on failed breakouts.
- **X3 (NO profit target - published doctrine):** the source explicitly rejects profit-taking: "Getting out of winning positions too early, i.e. 'taking a profit' too early, is one of the most common mistakes when trading trend following systems." (Ch. 6, p. 25.) → no profit-target exit is armed. The spread rides until X1, X2, or X4. This OVERRIDES every platform profit-ladder rule for this strategy.
- **X4 (time/roll - ADAPTED, futures-roll analog):** the published roll rule is for expiring futures: "As a general rule, the Turtles rolled existing positions into the new contract month a few weeks before expiration," and "don't roll into a new contract unless its price action would have resulted in an existing position." (Ch. 7, pp. 29–30.) ADAPTED: at 21 DTE ("a few weeks"), if neither X1 nor X2 has fired, close the spread; re-open the same-shape vertical in the next monthly expiry ONLY if the position would still be alive under X1/X2 (the published condition). Hold-to-expiry is NOT the doctrine.
- **Documented but NOT ARMED - Whipsaw alternate stop:** "the stops were placed at ½ N for ½% account risk. If a given Unit was stopped out, the Unit would be re-entered if the market reached the original entry price." (Ch. 5, p. 23.) Recorded for completeness; we run the standard 2N stop.

## 5. SIZING CONVENTION IN SOURCE

Volatility-normalized fixed-fractional units on a notional account: "Units were sized so that 1 N represented 1% of the account equity," i.e. "Unit = 1% of Account / (N × Dollars per Point)" (Ch. 3, p. 14). Risk cap: "The Turtles placed their stops based on position risk. No trade could incur more than 2% risk." (Ch. 5, p. 22.) Pyramiding to 4 Units per market at ½N spacing; portfolio caps 4/6/10/12 (single market / closely correlated / loosely correlated / single direction, p. 16). Drawdown de-leveraging: "decrease the size of the notional account by 20% each time we went down 10% of the original account." (p. 17.) **Recorded for context only - our shadow always runs exactly 1 published unit = one 1-lot debit vertical, account-blind, no pyramiding, no de-leveraging.**

## 6. DOCUMENTED PERFORMANCE

- Turtle program live results: "over the next four years, we earned an average annual compound rate of return of 80%." [verified-primary] (Introduction, p. 6.) Methodology caveat: aggregate lived experience of the 1984–1988 program on futures with pyramiding and notional accounts of "$500,000 to $2,000,000" [verified-primary] - not an audited track record and not a backtest of the exact rules as written.
- Independent backtest of System 1, original 21-market futures portfolio, 1970-01-01 → 2009-09-23 (Trading Blox; $1M start, 7% entry/exit slippage, $7 round-turn, Euro substituted for DM/FF, T-bill dropped, and - test modification - 1% risk per unit instead of the published 2%): CAGR 72% [verified-secondary]; max drawdown 66% [verified-secondary]; MAR ratio 1.09 [verified-secondary]; longest drawdown 85 months [verified-secondary]; 11,440 trades [verified-secondary]; average winner 43 calendar days, average loser 13 days [verified-secondary]. (Garner 2010, Tables 3–4.)
- Attenuation finding: "since the early 1990s, the system has essentially been unprofitable." [verified-secondary] (Garner 2010, p. 36.) ## 7. KNOWN FAILURE MODES

- **F1 - Capped right tail (adaptation-structural, severity: thesis-level).** The published expectancy comes from rare huge winners; the vertical caps every winner at (width − debit). If the cap is too tight the strategy inherits the Turtle loss rate without the Turtle payoff. Watch the ledger for win-size truncation against the underlying's post-breakout excursion.
- **F2 - Whipsaw/chop (published-structural).** "Most breakouts do not result in trends" (p. 25). Choppy ranges produce strings of 2N stop-outs / 10-day exits. The 1987-crash episode shows the tail risk of the futures form: "In some cases, 20% to 40% of account equity was lost in a single day" (p. 16) - our debit structure bounds this at the debit paid, one of the few places the adaptation is *safer* than the source.
- **F3 - Post-1990 attenuation (documented historical).** The secondary source found the 20/10 system "essentially been unprofitable" since the early 1990s with a 66% max DD and an 85-month drawdown [verified-secondary]. This strategy's published edge is the weakest-documented of the breakout family; treat it as a hypothesis under test, not a proven edge.
- **F4 - Theta bleed on slow trends (adaptation-structural).** A trend that grinds without hitting the 10-day exit still bleeds extrinsic value; the futures form paid no theta. The 45–75 DTE band and 21-DTE roll (X4) are the mitigations; grade them.
- **F5 - IV pop at entry (adaptation-structural).** Breakouts often coincide with vol expansion, inflating the debit exactly when the signal fires (buying high IV). The short leg partially hedges vega; still, entry debits on gap breakouts will be rich.
- **F6 - Signal clustering (mapping-structural).** Nine correlated equity names breach channels together (index regime moves); expect burst entries and correlated losses, unlike the 21-market published portfolio. The published correlation caps (6/10/12 units) are NOT armed under 1-lot; the exposure control is simply one spread per underlying.
- **F7 - Earnings gaps through the structure (mapping-structural).** Single-name breakouts (NVDA/TSLA/AMD especially) often precede earnings; a gap reversal blows through the 2N stop overnight - stop executes at open, worse than stop price (the source acknowledges gap fills: stops are exited when traded through, at market reality). Max loss stays bounded at debit.
- **F8 - Early assignment (options-structural, low for this shape).** The short leg of a debit vertical is OTM at entry; assignment risk arises only after deep favorable moves push it ITM, chiefly through ex-dividend dates on call spreads (SPY/IWM/QQQ quarterly ex-div; AAPL/MSFT/META small dividends). If short call is ITM with extrinsic < dividend at T-1 ex-div, close or roll early (PLATFORM-POLICY hygiene; the source has no analog).

## 9. DATA REQUIREMENTS

- **Daily history (underlying):** ≥ 80 trading days per name - 20/55-day entry channels, 10-day exit channel, N seed (20-day SMA of TR) plus EMA warm-up. Core requirement.
- **Intraday underlying marks:** the published trigger and both exits are intraday ("traded at the breakout when it was exceeded during the day"). 1-min bars ideal; 5–15-min polling acceptable - the levels (prior-day channel edges, frozen 2N stop) are known before the session, so detection is a threshold check, not a bar-shape question.
- **Tradier chains:** 30–90 DTE band per name (covers the 45–75 entry band plus roll targets), monthlies sufficient; need bid/ask/OI for both legs; strikes spanning spot ± ~3N.
- **Earnings calendar (date + bmo/amc):** observe-only logging (row 23) and F7/F8 hygiene - not an entry input.
- **FOMC/CPI dates:** not required - no published gate. Optional context logging only.
- **VIX regime / IV rank:** not required - no published gate (row 22). No IV-rank dependency, so the cold IV archive / VIX-percentile fallback is moot for this strategy.
- **Dividends/ex-div dates:** needed for the F8 early-assignment check on call verticals.
- **1-min bars:** only where intraday trigger detection uses them (above); not needed for grading.

## 10. SHADOW-IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

- **Expected trades/month:** ~2–5 across the 9-name universe, arriving in correlated bursts. Basis: the secondary backtest logged 11,440 trades over ~477 months on 21 markets *including* up-to-4-unit pyramiding → ≈ 6 breakout events/month ÷ 21 ≈ 0.29 events/market/month; × 9 names ≈ 2.6/month, before the winner-filter suppression and clustering. Estimate, not a sourced number. At ~3/month, an N≥25 verdict takes ≈ 8–9 months - slow lane; consider arming System 2 later only if the S1 lane proves out.
- **Mark cadence:** intraday underlying checks for trigger/X1/X2 (session-level threshold watch); option-leg marks needed at entry, exit, and daily EOD for the three-fill ledgers. No continuous Greek monitoring required.
- **Multi-leg shape:** 2-leg same-expiry debit vertical, 1 lot. Long leg ATM, short leg ~2N out (rows 17–18). Both legs opened and closed together, always as a spread ("All the Units in the position would be exited" - whole-position exits, p. 26).
- **Capital-at-risk basis:** debit paid (true max loss for a debit vertical). Not width-credit, not Reg-T proxy.
- **1-lot account-blind distortions vs published form (all deliberate):** no pyramiding (drops the published ½N add schedule - the source itself warns unit-adding speed materially changes results, p. 31), no volatility-normalized sizing (published Unit formula needs an account), no 4/6/10/12 correlation caps, no notional-account de-leveraging. The shadow grades the *signal + exit ladder in options form*, not Turtle portfolio management.
- **Exit-ladder override reminder:** X1 (10-day channel), X2 (2N stop), X3 (no profit target), X4 (21-DTE roll/close) replace the platform exit engine for this strategy. X3 in particular will look alien next to the platform's %-of-premium ladders - that is by design and by source doctrine.
- **Winner-filter state:** row 4 requires tracking the outcome of the last *hypothetical* breakout per underlying (taken or skipped) - persist per-name filter state (last breakout date, direction, resolved W/L per row 5 definition) in the strategy's state file; it cannot be reconstructed from fills alone.
