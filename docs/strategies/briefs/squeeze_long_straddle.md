Bollinger BandWidth Squeeze (six-month BandWidth low) anticipating a volatility breakout, expressed as a long ATM straddle.

## 2. PUBLISHED UNIVERSE & OUR MAPPING

**Published universe:** US single stocks and market indices on daily bars - the book's Squeeze/BandWidth worked examples are single names and index/bond markets (Clorox for BandWidth, Chart 8.4; Ch. 15 lists Squeeze examples "for the stock and bond markets," incl. PPL and Adobe per the book's own figure list). The author's site states the bands apply across asset classes. FJQ tests the same rules on 14 international stock indices.

**Our mapping:** SPY / QQQ / IWM plus the liquid mega-cap tier AAPL / NVDA / MSFT / TSLA / AMD / META. Signal computed on the underlying's daily bars exactly as published; position expressed as a 1-lot long ATM straddle.

**Why the mapping preserves the edge claim:** the published claim is instrument-generic - volatility is cyclical, and band compression to a six-month extreme precedes range expansion. The book demonstrates it on single stocks and indices alike; FJQ finds the same mechanics on indices. Our nine names are the most liquid option underlyings available, so the compression→expansion claim transfers; only the *expression* changes.

**ADAPTATIONS - DOCUMENTED LOUDLY:**
1. **Underlying breakout trade → long straddle.** The book's Method I is a DIRECTIONAL underlying trade: "wait for a Squeeze and go with the first breakout" (Ch. 16, archive snippet). The material read contains **no option-trading content** (archive Methods excerpt: 0 matches for option/straddle; mirror front chapters mention options only as historical context, never as a trade). Our straddle enters at the Squeeze (before direction resolves) and monetizes expansion either way. Deliberate rationale from the book's own text: the "head fake" is named as the "major problem" of Method I (Ch. 16) - a direction-neutral structure is immune to picking the wrong side of a head fake, at the cost of paying two premiums.
2. **Every option-structure constant (strike, DTE, timing, option exits) is ADAPTED or PLATFORM-POLICY** - the source publishes none of them. Section 8 tags each row; nothing option-shaped is attributed to Bollinger.
3. **Directional tools recorded but NOT ARMED:** the book's volume-indicator direction hints (Intraday Intensity / Accumulation Distribution) and half-position head-fake tactic are directional-entry machinery; a straddle doesn't use them. Recorded in Section 8 (row 23) so the directional variant can be armed later without re-research.

## 3. EXACT ENTRY RULES

- **Bands (signal substrate):** middle band = 20-day moving average; upper/lower = ±2 standard deviations (Bollinger 2001, p. 23 per FJQ; author's rules page: 20 periods, two standard deviations; archive snippet for Squeeze systems confirms "20-day average and +/- two standard deviation bands"). Simple MA per the book's default (mirror: Preface Table P.1 "Standard Bollinger Band Formulas"; Ch. 7 Construction compares simple vs exponential vs front-weighted with simple as the standard - corrected 2026-07-19, previously mis-cited as Ch. 3).
- **BandWidth:** "BandWidth = (Upper BB – Lower BB)/Middle BB" (formula as reproduced by FJQ from Bollinger 2001, p. 63; mirror text Ch. 8 Table 8.2 agrees).
- **Squeeze condition:** BandWidth at its six-month minimum. Book (archive snippet on the Squeeze lookback): "the default is six months"; FJQ's formalization of the book's rule: "(2) BandWidth drops to its six-month minimum." The book adds that lengthening the lookback yields greater compression and more explosive setups (archive snippet - corrected 2026-07-19: the remark sits in the excerpt's Ch. 16/Method I text, "recall that the default is six months", not Ch. 15) - recorded, not armed.
- **E1 (our entry trigger - ADAPTED timing):** on daily close, if BandWidth(20,2) equals the minimum of its trailing 126 trading sessions (including today) and the name is re-armed (row 20) and gates pass (rows 21–22, 24–25), signal fires. Enter the straddle the NEXT session (platform marketable-limit at spread mid). The book publishes no time-of-day; FJQ evaluates on daily closes. Entering AT the squeeze rather than at the subsequent band break is the deliberate straddle adaptation (Section 2, item 1); the book's own published trigger for the directional trade - first close outside a band - is recorded in row 9 and reused by our exit logic.
- **Strike selection:** NO PUBLISHED VALUE (no options in source). ADAPTED: single listed strike nearest spot, both legs (call + put) at that strike, same expiry. Echoes the practitioner options expression: "buying an ATM call and put" (Option Samurai 2024).
- **DTE selection:** NO PUBLISHED VALUE. ADAPTED: nearest monthly expiry in the 30–60 DTE band, preferring ~45. Rationale: the published anticipation→head-fake→breakout→expansion sequence plays out over weeks on 20-day bands; the expiry must outlive the expansion phase plus exit lag while keeping theta tolerable.
- **Published regime / IV gate:** NONE. The book's Squeeze machinery is price-only; no IV, VIX, or event filter appears in any portion read or in full-text searches. FJQ adds none. Any gate below is ours (rows 21–22).
- **Event timing (T-n):** NONE PUBLISHED. Platform policy adds an earnings exclusion (row 21) because a pre-earnings squeeze breaks the cheap-volatility premise - that is OUR reasoning, not the source's.

## 4. EXACT EXIT RULES - OVERRIDE ALL PLATFORM EXIT DOCTRINE FOR THIS STRATEGY

Published exit doctrine (for the book's directional breakout trade) and our straddle operationalization of it:

- **X1 (ride the expansion; trail out - published doctrine, ADAPTED application):** the book's Method I rides the post-Squeeze move with a trailing mechanism: "using a parabolic or opposite band tag stop" (archive snippet, Ch. 16 region); "a tag of the opposite band is an excellent exit signal" (archive snippet); starting the parabolic "under the most recent significant low" is discussed as a variant (corrected 2026-07-19: in the excerpt that discussion sits in the Method II chapter, which reuses "the same exit techniques" as Method I - same doctrine, different chapter). Straddle operationalization: once the underlying has closed outside a band (breakout resolved - row 9), hold; exit the whole straddle when the underlying tags the band OPPOSITE the breakout direction, or when a parabolic SAR trailing the breakout direction flips - whichever first. SAR constants are NOT published in the source (UNKNOWN); we run Wilder's defaults (row 16, ADAPTED).
- **X2 (no-expansion time stop - ADAPTED, no published analog):** if no daily close outside either band occurs within 15 trading sessions of entry, exit. The squeeze premise (imminent expansion) failed; a directional trader would simply have no entry yet, but a straddle bleeds theta, so this stop is structurally required by the adaptation. The 15 is OURS.
- **X3 (NO profit target - published absence, armed as doctrine):** no percent-of-premium or fixed profit target appears anywhere in the source's Squeeze/Method I material; the doctrine is to let the expansion run to the trailing exit. No profit-target rung is armed. This OVERRIDES the platform's %-of-premium ladders for this strategy.
- **X4 (NO premium stop-loss - published absence + structure):** no stop-loss on the trade's value is published; the long straddle's maximum loss is the debit paid, which is the platform capital-at-risk basis (row 27). No premium stop armed.
- **X5 (DTE floor - PLATFORM-POLICY):** close at ≤ 7 DTE regardless of state. Ours: avoids gamma/pin/exercise operations; hold-to-expiry is NOT published doctrine anywhere in the source.
- **Roll rules:** NONE PUBLISHED, none armed. One squeeze episode = one straddle, then re-arm per row 20.

## 6. DOCUMENTED PERFORMANCE

Primary book: NO quantitative backtest statistics for The Squeeze or Method I found in any portion read or via full-text search - the book is worked-example-driven. Book-level performance: **UNKNOWN**.

FJQ (directional underlying rules on 14 international stock indices; Squeeze = band break + six-month BandWidth minimum; next-day close returns; no options):

## 7. KNOWN FAILURE MODES

- **F1 - Head fake (published-structural).** "The major problem with successfully implementing Method I is something called a head fake" (Ch. 16, archive snippet) - price breaks one way, reverses hard the other. Directionally our straddle is immune (either resolution pays), BUT a head fake that reverses into renewed compression - a fake with no follow-through expansion - leaves the straddle bleeding theta with no move. X2 is the mitigation.
- **F2 - Post-publication attenuation (documented historical).** FJQ: Squeeze predictive in only 1 of 14 index markets after 2001, and predictability decayed first in the US, where the bands were introduced [verified-secondary]. The directional edge on indices is essentially dead post-publication. Partial transfer caveat: our P&L is realized-vol expansion, which FJQ did not measure - but this is the single strongest documented warning about the family.
- **F3 - Compression regimes / vol grind (named episode: 2017 US low-vol regime).** BandWidth can make successive six-month lows for months (2017 SPX realized-vol floor is the canonical episode; SPY squeezes fired repeatedly into ever-lower vol). Straddles bought at each new low bleed. Re-arm hysteresis (row 20) throttles, does not eliminate.
- **F4 - IV-not-cheap trap (adaptation-structural).** The cheap-options logic assumes implied follows the realized compression. In single names squeezing into known events (earnings especially), IV is already marked up - the straddle pays fair-or-rich for the move and eats post-event vol crush. Row 21 excludes earnings-proximate entries; FOMC/CPI proximity on index names is logged observe-only.
- **F5 - Vol-crush on resolution (adaptation-structural).** After the breakout resolves, IV often deflates even as the trend continues; the trailing exit (X1) lags by construction and gives back vega. Grade X1 lag cost in the ledger.
- **F6 - Two-leg friction (adaptation-structural).** Double bid-ask spread at entry and exit on a debit structure whose documented underlying-signal margin is thin (F2, and QuantifiedStrategies' failure to beat buy-and-hold on the underlying [verified-secondary]).
- **F7 - Signal clustering (mapping-structural).** SPY/QQQ/IWM (and the mega-caps with them) compress and fire together in index vol regimes; expect correlated entries and correlated F3 losses, not nine independent bets.
- **F8 - Early assignment: NOT APPLICABLE.** Both legs are long; there is no short leg to be assigned, no ex-div risk. Pin risk only arises if held into expiry, which X5 forbids.
