## 2. PUBLISHED UNIVERSE & OUR MAPPING

- **What the source trades:** tastylive teaches the jade lizard on generic single "stocks/ETFs" (primary page: "identify a potential underlying with which you have a neutral or slightly bullish outlook", example on hypothetical $50 stock XYZ; their From Theory to Practice portfolio traded e.g. an "XLE Jade Lizard", Dec 3 2018 episode description). Secondaries use AMZN, ROKU, NVDA. This is an equity/ETF options strategy, not an index-cash-settled (SPX) product.
- **Our mapping:** SPY / QQQ / IWM plus the liquid mega-cap tier AAPL / NVDA / MSFT / TSLA / AMD / META. One published unit = 1 short put + 1 short call + 1 long call, same expiration, 1 contract each.
- **Why the mapping preserves the edge claim:** the published edge is (a) equity volatility risk premium harvested with (b) the put-vs-call skew asymmetry - "A Jade Lizard can exploit skew in the market in a way that is unmatched by any other strategy" (tastylive episode description, Jan 8 2022) - and (c) structural removal of upside risk via credit > call-spread width. All three are properties of any liquid equity/ETF chain with normal put skew; SPY/QQQ/IWM and the mega-caps have the tightest spreads and penny/nickel increments, which makes the credit-exceeds-width condition *easier* to satisfy at fair prices than on the average stock, so the mapping is conservative with respect to the claim.
- **ADAPTATION AUDIT (options-only platform):** the jade lizard is already a pure 3-leg options structure - **no stock legs and no delta-hedging are required by the published form**. The only stock-related element in the source is *assignment* language ("The option seller may be obligated to buy the underlying asset at the put's strike price"); our shadow platform never takes assignment, so an ITM short leg at expiry is closed/marked at intrinsic instead. Flagged again in section 10.

## 4. EXACT EXIT RULES

These override platform exit doctrine for this strategy.

- **Profit target - close at 50% of max profit:** "The first profit target is generally 50% of the maximum profit. This is done by buying the strangle back for 50% of the credit received at order entry." [tastylive strangle doctrine page - same doctrine-transfer caveat as DTE]. Jade-lizard-specific secondary confirmation: "If you collect 50% of the original premium, you should take the trade off for a profit, according to Tastytrade." [FinancialTechWiz jade lizard guide, verified-secondary]. Implementation: close all three legs when position can be bought back for ≤ 50% of the opening net credit.
- **Time exit - manage at 21 DTE:** tastylive ran a lizard-specific study titled "Jade Lizard: Managing at 21 DTE" (Market Measures, Apr 1 2021), description verbatim: "tastylive analyzes the performance of the Jade Lizard by applying managing at 21 DTE strategy." The episode's internal numbers are no longer retrievable (page removed) → the *verdict* is UNKNOWN; the *mechanic* (21 DTE as the management checkpoint) is the documented tastylive doctrine, restated by secondaries: "The 21 DTE rule states that options sellers should close or roll their positions when they reach 21 days to expiration (DTE), regardless of current profit or loss." [daystoexpiry.com, Feb 15 2026, verified-secondary]. Implementation: if the 50% target has not hit by 21 DTE, close all legs at 21 DTE.
- **Loss management (no stop-loss published):** tastylive publishes **no fixed stop-loss** for the jade lizard - do not invent one. Their defensive doctrine is rolling: "rolling the untested side (non-losing side) closer to the stock price when our tested side (losing side) is breached is optimal" [tastylive strangle doctrine page]; jade-lizard-specific: "If the stock shoots through the call credit spread, you can roll the short put up to collect more premium" [FinancialTechWiz, verified-secondary]. Also loss-management study existence: "Does managing losses make sense when trading Jade Lizards?" [Mar 9 2016 episode description; verdict content UNKNOWN].
- **Roll rules (expiry):** "Rolling involves closing out the existing options and simultaneously re-opening the position with a later expiration date." [primary, "How to Exit"]. Our shadow does **not** roll (see ADAPTED in section 8): the trade is a unit - enter once, exit by 50% target or 21 DTE, whichever comes first. Rolls would create chained positions our grading ledger treats as new entries anyway.
- **Hold-to-expiry doctrine:** not the doctrine. The primary lists natural expiration only as one possible outcome ("the option seller can let the options expire without taking any further action"), but the tastylive management approach (50% / 21 DTE) supersedes it; our implementation never holds inside 21 DTE.

## 7. KNOWN FAILURE MODES

- **Downside gap/crash (the risk leg is the naked short put):** Feb-5-2018 Volmageddon, Feb-Mar-2020 COVID crash, Aug-5-2024 VIX spike - all are short-equity-put loss episodes; the call-spread credit only buffers the first ~credit dollars of a decline ("if the stock moves lower, some of the losses from the short put will be offset by the gains from the short call spread" [primary] - the offset is capped at the credit). Max downside loss ≈ put strike minus total credit, realized if the underlying goes to zero.
- **Adverse selection of the entry gate:** the credit > width condition is easiest to satisfy exactly when IV is elevated ("In elevated IV, you have more room." [TradeStation]) - so entries concentrate in high-vol regimes, i.e. immediately before the fat left tail is most live. This is the same regime clustering that hurt every short-vol family in the named episodes above.
- **"No upside risk" holds only at expiration:** an early sharp rally inflates the short call spread's mark faster than the put decays; mark-to-market drawdown on the upside is possible mid-trade even though expiry P&L up there is positive. Our intraday marks will show this; it is path risk, not a bug.
- **Single-name earnings gaps (AAPL/NVDA/MSFT/TSLA/AMD/META tier):** a gap through the short put strike skips all management; hence the ADAPTED earnings exclusion.
- **Early assignment:** short call (lower leg of the spread) that goes ITM near an ex-dividend date on SPY/IWM/QQQ or dividend payers (AAPL/MSFT/META/NVDA pay) can be assigned early; deep-ITM short puts can be assigned any time. Shadow platform takes no assignment - real-world replication risk, documented not modeled.
- **Pin risk at expiry:** avoided structurally because we always exit by 21 DTE ("Consider closing positions before expiration if the underlying is near either short strike" [TradeStation]).
- **Width-scaling trap:** the published $1-$5 width band is calibrated to $50-$500 stocks; on a $560 SPY a $1 spread is degenerate (long call nearly free, credit>width trivially satisfied but upside protection meaningless). See width row in section 8.

## 10. SHADOW-IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

- **Expected trades/month:** ~2-6, expected ~4. Ceiling: 9 symbols x one position per ~45-day cycle ≈ 6/month if every symbol passed every cycle. The VIX-percentile≥50 gate plus the hard credit>width gate (hard to satisfy in quiet tape, per TradeStation: elevated IV gives "more room") will reject cycles in low-vol regimes - expect clustering: near-zero entries in calm months, near-ceiling in vol spikes. Grading timeline: at ~4/month, N≥25 per-lane verdicts need ~6+ months; the pooled e-process wealth accumulates faster across symbols.
- **Mark cadence:** platform-standard intraday polling of all 3 leg mids (position mark = short put mid + short call mid - long call mid vs opening credit) to catch the 50% buyback trigger intraday; EOD mark mandatory for the ledgers. No greeks-based triggers needed after entry.
- **Multi-leg shape:** 3 legs, same expiry: SELL 1 OTM put (~0.30Δ), SELL 1 OTM call (~0.20Δ), BUY 1 call one width higher. Enter as one combo at combined mid (fill-realism haircut per platform convention); exit likewise as one combo.
- **Capital-at-risk basis:** width-credit is WRONG for this family (upside is riskless by construction when gate 8 passes); use the cash-secured-put proxy: **put strike x 100 - net credit** per unit (row 19). This matches the primary's own max-loss arithmetic and keeps CaR comparable with any other short-put-family lane.
- **Account-blind 1-lot distortions:** (a) the source's short put is "typically cash-secured" - sizing/margin realism is out of scope for the shadow, so our returns-on-CaR will look conservative vs a margin account's buying-power math; (b) no assignment modeling: ITM legs at exit are marked at quote/intrinsic - real accounts face early assignment (esp. short call through ex-div) that we only document (section 7); (c) no rolling (row 17): our P&L will diverge from a tastylive-style managed account precisely in the losing tail, where they roll and we realize the 21-DTE mark. That divergence direction is unknown a priori - tastylive's own Mar-9-2016 loss-management study verdict is UNKNOWN (content removed) - so do not assume the roll-free variant is worse; let the grader decide.
- **Exit-doctrine override reminder:** rows 14-16 OVERRIDE the platform exit ladder for this strategy id, per the standing strategy-brief convention. No platform stop-loss may be silently attached; if risk appetite demands one later, it must be registered as a PLATFORM-POLICY amendment to row 16, never attributed to the source.
