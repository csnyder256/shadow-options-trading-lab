# Strategy Brief: backspread_1x2

Call (or put) 1x2 backspread - sell 1 closer-to-the-money option, buy 2 further-OTM options of the same type and expiration, for a net credit or even money; direction chosen by which way a large move is expected.
Researched 2026-07-19. PRIMARY SOURCE READ: McMillan 5th ed. backspread chapter read in full (OCR full text of the archive.org scan, grepped and read line-by-line: Ch. 13 pp. 232–235, Ch. 14 pp. 240–241, Ch. 37 pp. 781–782, Ch. 39 pp. 840–844, Ch. 34 pp. 686–688). CO-PRIMARY READ: Natenberg 1994 Ch. 8 backspread section pp. 137–139 + IV-selection passage p. 166 (OCR full text of the archive.org scan). Secondary (Options Playbook call + put backspread pages) READ.

**PROVENANCE HEADLINE:** the published doctrine is qualitative-rich but number-poor. What IS published: the 1x2 shape, the credit/even-money requirement, the ~2:1 delta relationship between the legs, the low-IV-percentile entry gate, the direction-selection rule, the skew rule, and a small set of qualitative exits (no stop, hold-to-expiry frame, discretionary profit-take on a favorable move, early-assignment vigilance). What is NOT published anywhere in the sources: DTE, time of day, numeric IV threshold, numeric profit target, numeric stop, numeric time exit, and any backtest performance. Every such gap is recorded as UNKNOWN below with an explicitly-tagged platform default where one is operationally required. Zero invented constants are attributed to the sources.

## 2. PUBLISHED UNIVERSE & OUR MAPPING

**Published universe:** McMillan Ch. 13 develops the backspread on generic single-stock options ("XYZ"); Ch. 39 applies the call backspread to **OEX index options** explicitly to harvest reverse volatility skew; Ch. 34 applies the put backspread to soybean futures options. Natenberg Ch. 8 is instrument-generic (his tables are futures-style at 100). The strategy is published as venue-generic.

**Our mapping:** SPY / QQQ / IWM plus the liquid mega-cap tier AAPL / NVDA / MSFT / TSLA / AMD / META. Index-proxy ETFs stand in for OEX (same reverse-skew structure McMillan exploits in Ch. 39); single names carry the "volatile stock / pre-event" use (McMillan p. 235; Playbook tips).

**Why the mapping preserves the edge claim:** the published claim is structural, not venue-specific - limited-risk long convexity financed by a nearer strike, profitable on a large move either way when opened at a credit, with a statistical assist when IV is in a low percentile and (on the call side, indices) when short leg IV > long leg IV via reverse skew. All of that transfers to the most liquid US option underlyings; nothing in the published form depends on OEX or soybeans specifically.

**ADAPTATIONS - DOCUMENTED LOUDLY:**
1. **Fixed 1x2 vs delta-neutral ratio.** Both sources' *general* form sizes the ratio off deltas (McMillan p. 234: neutral ratio 0.80/0.35 = 2.29:1; Natenberg p. 138 defines the backspread as delta-neutral). Both sources ALSO publish the exact 1x2 as their worked example - McMillan p. 233 (buy 2 / sell 1) and p. 842, where deltas 0.40/0.20 make the neutral spread precisely "Buy 2 … Sell 1." We freeze the ratio at 1x2 and instead enforce the delta relationship at entry (row 7), which is the published example made systematic. McMillan's caveat (p. 235) that a convenience 2:1 can under-shoot neutrality is recorded; no continuous re-hedging is performed (options-only platform; neither source's backspread chapter requires stock legs - none exist in the published form).
2. **"Even money" boundary.** McMillan's same-expiry Ch. 13 rule demands a credit; the literal even-money case is published in his Ch. 14 diagonal variant ("This would be an even money spread," p. 240). Our gate accepts credit-or-even and rejects debits (row 5) - the strict Ch. 13 posture, with the even-money boundary from Ch. 14. The Playbook secondary tolerates "a small net debit" - recorded, NOT armed.
3. **No numeric DTE / profit target / stop is published** - every armed number of that kind below is tagged ADAPTED or PLATFORM-POLICY.

## 3. EXACT ENTRY RULES

- **Structure (call side):** sell 1 call at strike K1, buy 2 calls at strike K2 > K1, same expiration. McMillan p. 232: one would "sell a call at one striking price" and buy several calls "at a higher striking price"; worked example p. 233: Buy 2 July 45 calls, Sell 1 July 40 call (stock 43). Natenberg p. 138: a call backspread is "long calls at a higher exercise price," short calls at the lower.
- **Structure (put side, direction-adaptive mirror):** sell 1 put at K2, buy 2 puts at K1 < K2, same expiration. Natenberg p. 138: "long puts at a lower exercise price," short puts at the higher; McMillan Ch. 34 pp. 686–687 (neutral-ratio put backspread, 15-cent credit).
- **Same expiration:** all legs one expiry. Natenberg p. 138: "all options expire at the same time."
- **Net-cost trigger (the defining gate):** net premium ≥ 0 (credit or even money); reject debits. McMillan p. 233: "These spreads are generally established for credits," and "if the spread cannot be initiated at a credit, it is usually not attractive." Natenberg p. 138: "Typically, a backspread is done for a credit." Even-money boundary: McMillan p. 240, "The credits equal the debits when the position is established."
- **Strike selection / delta algorithm:** short leg at-the-money to slightly ITM (stock at/around K1 - McMillan's example short is 3 pts ITM, p. 233; Playbook: stock "at or around strike price A"); long legs further OTM such that **delta_short ≈ 2 × delta_long**. McMillan p. 842 worked example: short 590 call delta 0.40, long 600 calls delta 0.20 - buy 2 / sell 1 "since the deltas are in the ratio of 2-to-1." Operationally: pick the ≈0.40Δ strike to sell and the ≈0.20Δ strike to buy, then walk the long strike inward until net premium ≥ 0 or fail (order of operations is OURS - row 8 note).
- **Direction selection (the "direction-adaptive" part):** call side when the thesis is a large up-move, put side when a large down-move. Natenberg p. 139: with "great upside potential" choose a call backspread; with great downside potential, the put backspread. Skew overlay for index tickers: McMillan Ch. 39 p. 840 lists selling nearer/higher-IV calls and buying more OTM lower-IV calls as the backspread that harvests reverse (index) skew; the put backspread is his prescription for **forward**-skew markets (p. 843–844) - so on SPY/QQQ/IWM the put side pays a skew tax and requires a stronger directional thesis (row 10).
- **IV regime gate (published, qualitative):** enter when implied volatility is in a **low percentile**. McMillan p. 841 (rule of thumb): the backspread "would be used if implied volatility were in a low percentile," so drift back "toward the 50th percentile" helps the position; the ratio spread is the high-percentile counterpart. Natenberg p. 166: when options are underpriced (low IV), "look for spreads with a positive vega" - backspread category. **No numeric percentile is published** → operational default row 12, tagged ADAPTED.
- **Underlying/catalyst condition:** volatile underlying or a scheduled catalyst; avoid quiet names. McMillan p. 235: "seek out volatile stocks" for this strategy; Natenberg p. 139: "He will avoid backspreads in quiet markets." Event flavor (secondary): Playbook tip - a trade to "consider just prior to a major news event" (FDA, legal, patent examples). Published T-n: NONE → row 15.
- **DTE selection:** **UNKNOWN - no DTE is published in either textbook.** Secondary qualitative only (Playbook: "using longer-term options to give the stock more time to move"). Platform default row 17, tagged ADAPTED.
- **Entry timing (time of day):** **UNKNOWN - nothing published.** Standard funnel scan window applies (PLATFORM-POLICY, row 18).

## 4. EXACT EXIT RULES - OVERRIDE ALL PLATFORM EXIT DOCTRINE FOR THIS STRATEGY

Published doctrine is: structure-limited risk, minimal management, expiration-framed payoff, with three named discretionary actions.

- **X1 - Default: hold toward expiration.** All published max-profit/max-loss/breakeven arithmetic is "at expiration" (McMillan pp. 233–234, Table 13-1; Natenberg Figs. 8-1/8-2 are expiration-value graphs). McMillan p. 235: risk being "limited by the nature of the position," there is "little in the way of defensive follow-up action" required. **No stop-loss exists in the published form** - none is armed.
- **X2 - Discretionary profit-take on the favorable move (no numeric target published):** McMillan p. 235: he "might take profits by closing the spread" if the stock rallies before expiration. Operationalization (ADAPTED, row 19): close when the spread's mark reaches the published worst-case loss magnitude in profit, i.e. net mark ≥ (K2−K1) − credit - a symmetric, non-invented anchor derived from the source's own max-loss quantity; tagged ADAPTED because the source gives no number.
- **X3 - Edge-gone profit-take:** if the entry edge (skew/IV advantage) disappears while profitable, take the profit. McMillan p. 844: if the skew disappears with the position on, a profit generally results and it would "behoove the strategist to take the profit."
- **X4 - Backspread-family roll (recorded, NOT ARMED):** McMillan p. 844 names, for backspreads, "the taking of partial profits and possibly rolling the long options" to a more at-the-money strike. At 1-lot (2 long contracts) partial-profit mechanics are distorted - recorded for a future multi-lot variant, not armed (Section 10).
- **X5 - Early-assignment guard (published vigilance, our numeric):** McMillan p. 235: the strategist must be "alert to the possibility of early exercise," having sold an ITM option. Armed guard (ADAPTED, row 23): if the short leg is ITM and its extrinsic value ≤ $0.05 with an ex-dividend date (calls) before expiry, or ≤ $0.05 any time inside 5 DTE, close the whole spread. The trigger's existence is published; the $0.05/5-DTE numbers are OURS.
- **Time exit:** NONE PUBLISHED. No time stop armed; X1 governs. (Contrast with platform ladders - explicitly overridden here.)

## 5. SIZING CONVENTION IN SOURCE

McMillan sizes per-spread by collateral: the embedded bear-spread requires the strike-width difference, and the credit received "can be applied against the initial requirement" - worked example p. 234: $500 width requirement − $200 credit = **$300 net requirement**, with "no increase or decrease" since nothing is naked. Ch. 37 p. 782 adds the only exposure warning: a fall in IV is not catastrophic "unless one had overcommitted his funds" to one position - i.e., don't concentrate; no %-of-buying-power figure is published. Natenberg publishes contract-count examples only (delta-neutral ratios), no account sizing. **Our shadow always runs exactly 1 published unit = one 1x2 spread, account-blind.**

## 7. KNOWN FAILURE MODES

- **F1 - Terminal pin at the long strike (published-structural, the defining failure).** Max loss lands exactly at K2 at expiration: "the maximum loss is attained at expiration at the striking price of the purchased call" (McMillan p. 233; Table 13-1 shows −$300 at 45 vs +$200 below 40). A slow drift that dies at the long strike is the worst possible path.
- **F2 - Quiet market / theta bleed (published-structural).** Natenberg p. 138: "If no movement occurs, a backspread is likely to be a losing strategy"; p. 139: avoid quiet markets. A credit-open caps this loss at width−credit but the position bleeds toward F1 daily.
- **F3 - IV contraction (published-structural).** McMillan p. 781–782: rising IV benefits, falling IV is "slightly harmful"; Natenberg p. 153 (swift move or rising IV helps - reversed). Sharpest expression: post-event vol crush when an event entry (earnings/FOMC) resolves without a large move - the long 0.20Δ wings deflate hardest. (Event-crush framing is OUR structural analysis; the IV-direction sensitivity is published.)
- **F4 - Early assignment on the short ITM leg (published-structural).** McMillan p. 235's early-exercise alert; mechanically worst through ex-dividend for short ITM calls (ex-div mechanism = standard market structure, our note). Put side: early assignment on short ITM puts after big down moves.
- **F5 - Put-side skew tax on index tickers (published-structural).** Reverse index skew means the put backspread buys the expensive high-IV OTM puts and sells the cheaper nearer put - the exact opposite of McMillan's sell-rich/buy-cheap skew logic (Ch. 39 pp. 840–844, where put backspreads are prescribed only for forward-skew markets like soybeans). Direction-adaptive use on SPY/QQQ/IWM must expect negative structural edge on the put side.
- **F6 - Low-vol melt-up regimes (historical, OUR analysis - not source-attributed).** 2017-style and mid-2023-style index grinds are the F1/F2/F3 trifecta for call backspreads: steady upward drift into the long strike with falling IV. Note the family's *famous* vol episodes (Feb-2018, Mar-2020, Aug-2024) are WIN scenarios for open backspreads, not failures - the danger regime is calm, not storm.
- **F7 - Fill/width friction on ratio structures (platform-structural).** Three contracts, two strikes, and an even-money target make entry highly sensitive to spread width; a "credit on paper" at mids can be a debit at touch. Liquidity gates (row 26) and the credit-or-even hard gate protect the published premise.

## 10. SHADOW-IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

- **Expected trades/month:** ~4–8, plan on **6**. Drivers: 9 underlyings × (low-IV-percentile gate + catalyst/volatility screen + credit-or-even fill gate). The credit gate is the real throttle - at low IV, even-money 1x2s at 0.40Δ/0.20Δ are only intermittently available (skew and width dependent). Grading timeline: N≥25 in ~4–5 months pooled; note the direction-adaptive split means the put lane on index tickers will be rare (row 10) - grade pooled first, per-side later.
- **Mark cadence:** daily EOD net-mid mark on the 2-leg package (3 contracts); extra mark at next open after any event date inside the position's life; X2/X3/assignment-guard evaluated daily at EOD scan.
- **Multi-leg shape:** 2 legs / 3 contracts, same type, same expiry, ratio 1:2 (short nearer strike × 1, long further strike × 2). No stock legs, no re-hedging - matches the published retail form exactly; the professional delta-neutral variant (2.29:1 etc.) is documented in Section 2 as deliberately not implemented.
- **Capital-at-risk basis:** **width-minus-credit** - CaR = (K2−K1)×100 − net credit. This equals both the published expiration max loss (McM5 p. 233) and the published collateral requirement (p. 234: width requirement less credit received), so the Reg-T proxy and the economic max loss coincide for this family. Do NOT use debit-paid (there is none) or naked-margin proxies.
- **1-lot account-blind distortions:** (a) partial-profit/roll doctrine (row 24) is un-executable at 1-lot without closing the asymmetric halves - recorded, not armed; (b) the fixed 1x2 slightly under-weights the long side whenever delta_short > 2×delta_long - the row 7 entry filter bounds this drift; (c) McMillan's only sizing warning (don't overcommit to one position, p. 782) is automatically satisfied at 1 unit.
- **Sign convention warning for the ledger:** this family opens at a CREDIT but is a LONG-convexity position - do not let %-of-premium exit machinery (built for debit longs or short-premium) touch it; Section 4 overrides apply.
