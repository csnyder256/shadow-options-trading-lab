## 2. PUBLISHED UNIVERSE & OUR MAPPING

- **What the sources trade:** tastylive teaches the structure generically for "high implied volatility (IV) environments" in liquid optionable underlyings (their worked example is an abstract $100-class stock). Options Trading IQ demonstrates on SPY and MSFT. Data Driven Options trades it on "index options or options on index funds" - explicitly avoiding single stocks because they carry "tail risk, or make big moves beyond what is expected" [verified-secondary, DDO page].
- **Our mapping:** SPY/QQQ/IWM (primary tier - closest to the published index-fund/SPY habitat) plus the liquid mega-cap tier AAPL/NVDA/MSFT/TSLA/AMD/META (secondary tier - sanctioned by the OTIQ MSFT worked example, but note DDO's explicit single-stock warning).
- **Why the mapping preserves the edge claim:** the edge claim is structural, not symbol-specific - a wider put credit spread fully finances a narrower put debit spread sharing the short strike, so a rally, a pin, or a modest selloff all win; only a crash through the wide wing loses. That payoff geometry transfers to any liquid penny-strike chain. Index ETFs preserve it best (no earnings gaps); single names carry documented extra gap risk (see section 7) and are gated by an earnings filter (PLATFORM-POLICY).
- **No stock legs, no delta-hedging needed:** the published form is a pure 3-leg / 4-lot options structure. No adaptation of that kind required. The LOUD adaptations here are instead: (a) DTE=45 imported from general tastylive doctrine because the primary BWB page is silent; (b) delta targets imported from secondary put-BWB specs published at shorter DTE (see section 3).

## 3. EXACT ENTRY RULES

- **Structure (put side):** buy 1 put above the short strike (narrow debit spread), sell 2 puts at the middle strike, buy 1 put further below (wide credit spread). Primary defines the BWB as "a long butterfly spread with long strikes that aren't equidistant from the short strike" and requires that "the debit spread portion of the trade is more narrow than the credit spread portion" [verified-primary]. ShadowTrader corroborates: one long and one short vertical "being at the same strike" on the short side, short vertical wider [verified-secondary].
- **Credit trigger (the defining gate):** "We always route this for a credit." - primary, verbatim. Rationale, primary: doing so "eliminate[s] the risk of losing money if the entire spread expires OTM" [verified-primary]. OTIQ states the put-side consequence: entered for a net credit "there is no risk on the upside" [verified-secondary]. **Entry rule: net credit must be > $0 after assumed fill at mid or better; otherwise no trade.**
- **Strike-selection algorithm:**
  - Primary publishes the structural rule and one numeric example (call side): buy 1 × $120C / sell 2 × $105C / buy 1 × $100C for a $1.00 net credit - i.e., narrow wing 5 wide, broken wing 15 wide (3:1) [verified-primary]. **No delta targets are published on the primary page.**
  - Secondary put-BWB deltas: DDO uses "32/28/20" - buy ~32Δ put, sell 2 × ~28Δ puts, buy ~20Δ put, with the far wing "twice as far away from the body as the expensive wing" (2:1) [verified-secondary, published for their 21-DTE variant]. The DDO companion article variant: "Sell two of the 25 delta puts and buy one a strike or two above, and buy the one twice as far below" [verified-secondary].
  - **Our implementation (ADAPTED, loudly):** short strike ≈ 25–30Δ put (default 28Δ), upper long 1–2 strikes above the short, lower long placed so the wide wing is 2× the narrow wing width. Deltas and ratio come from the secondary put-BWB specs above, not from the primary page, and those specs were published at 17–21 DTE, not 45. The 3:1 ratio from the primary's example is retained as the allowed upper bound of the range.
- **Credit-size floor (secondary, optional-strict):** DDO: "I will only enter a trade if I can collect 10% or more of the width of the narrow wing" [verified-secondary]. Adopted as our entry floor (ADAPTED - published for the 21-DTE variant; at 45 DTE this floor is easier to meet, making it a conservative-to-neutral import).
- **DTE selection:** primary BWB page: silent. tastylive general doctrine via Perlman: "For 45-day options, there is a level of predictability and lower risk during the initial period after trade initiation. This supports a strategy of early management, around the 21-day mark" [verified-secondary - Traders Reserve quoting tastylive research]. **Our rule: enter in the expiration nearest 45 DTE (accept 35–50).** Tagged ADAPTED. Counter-evidence on 45 DTE for standard flies is recorded in section 6.
- **Entry timing (time of day):** UNKNOWN - no source publishes an intraday entry time. (DDO publishes a day-of-week preference for their 21-DTE variant: "Ideally, I open this trade on a Friday" [verified-secondary] - not imported.) Platform default scan window applies (PLATFORM-POLICY).
- **Regime / IV gate:** primary: the strategy is for "high implied volatility (IV) environments", targeting "high IV rank and a high IV%" [verified-primary] - **but no numeric threshold is published. Threshold = UNKNOWN.** Platform proxy (our archive's IV-rank history is cold): VIX 1-year percentile ≥ 50 at entry, tagged PLATFORM-POLICY, explicitly NOT attributed to tastylive.
- **Event gates:** none published in any cited source. Earnings-exclusion for single names is ours (PLATFORM-POLICY, section 8).

## 4. EXACT EXIT RULES (override platform exit doctrine for this strategy)

- **Profit target:** primary publishes both "50% of our max profit" ("We usually aim for 50% of our max profit on the trade.") and a more conservative alternative ("We like to aim for 25% of the max profit on the trade.") [verified-primary]. **Our rule: close the whole structure at 50% of max profit (range 25–50%, documented default = 50%).** Primary also gives the shortcut for locating the profit checkpoints: take the distance between the closest long option and the short options and divide by two (50%) or four (25%) [verified-primary, paraphrase of page text].
- **Defensive management (trade moving against us, i.e., selloff toward/through the shorts):** primary, verbatim: "If the trade goes against you, we will look to close our long spread aspect of the trade for max profit, and potentially roll the remaining short spread out in time if we can do so for a credit." [verified-primary]. Shadow implementation: when the underlying trades through the short strike, close the narrow debit spread; the residual short put spread is then managed as a rolled position only if a roll to the next cycle is available for a credit, else closed at the 21-DTE checkpoint.
- **Time exit:** primary BWB page: silent. tastylive general doctrine: manage "around the 21-day mark, to lock in gains and reduce exposure" [verified-secondary, Perlman via Traders Reserve; also the tastylive Market Measures segment "Why We Manage at 21 DTE", https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sqh0u63Zw6o]. **Our rule (ADAPTED): if neither the profit target nor the defensive trigger has fired by 21 DTE, close the entire structure at the mark.** No position is ever held into expiration week (corroborating secondary: OTIQ advises to "Close the position before expiration week" [verified-secondary]).
- **Stop loss:** **UNKNOWN - the primary publishes no stop loss**; its published loss doctrine is the defensive close-and-roll above plus the structural cap ("capped at the furthest OTM long option" [verified-primary]). Secondary alternatives on record, NOT adopted: OTIQ "Some traders like to set a stop loss at 20% of capital at risk. Others might set it as 30%." [verified-secondary]; DDO comments discuss "2-3x the initial credit received" [verified-secondary, informal]. We follow the primary: no fixed stop; max loss is the structure's defined risk; the 21-DTE exit is the hard backstop.
- **Hold-to-expiry doctrine:** explicitly NOT hold-to-expiry (see time exit).

## 7. KNOWN FAILURE MODES

- **Left-tail crash through the wide wing** - the whole loss zone lives below the shorts; a fast gap through both lower strikes realizes near-max loss with no chance to execute the published defensive roll. Named episodes of exactly this tape: Feb-2018 Volmageddon, Mar-2020 COVID crash, Aug-5-2024 vol spike. DDO's own warning: months of wins, then "a situation that wipes out everything earned" [verified-secondary].
- **Vol expansion marks the position against you early** - the structure is short net premium in high IV; an IV spike inflates the mark loss well before strikes are breached, which stresses any mark-to-market grader even when the trade would have recovered.
- **Whipsaw around the defensive trigger** - closing the debit spread on a breach converts the position to a naked-ish short put spread; a V-bottom then leaves you short into a rally with the hedge gone. The published roll-for-credit is not always available.
- **Single-name gap risk** - earnings/news gaps blow through both lower strikes in one print (why DDO restricts to indexes [verified-secondary]). Our mega-cap tier is exposed; mitigated by the earnings-exclusion gate (PLATFORM-POLICY).
- **Early assignment on the short puts** - American-style ETF/equity options: short puts assigned when deep ITM (extrinsic ≈ 0), most likely after a crash - exactly when spreads are widest. No ex-div mechanism for puts (that's the call-side failure mode), but hard-to-borrow/interest-driven put assignment exists. The 21-DTE exit and no-expiration-week rule reduce but do not eliminate this.
- **Pin/expiration-week gamma at the shorts** - published mitigation: exit before expiration week (OTIQ [verified-secondary]) and 21-DTE management; we never carry into that window.
- **Fill/liquidity reality of 3-leg orders** - a "credit" at mid may be unfillable; the whole edge claim collapses if entry is actually at a debit. Shadow fill-ledger discipline (three-fill marks) applies.

## 10. SHADOW-IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

- **Expected trades/month:** 9 symbols × ~1.2 possible cycles/month (45→21 DTE hold ≈ 24 days), throttled by the credit gate (row 9–10) and IV gate (row 11) which will reject many scans in low-vol tape → realistic estimate **4–6 entries/month, plan on 5**. Grading timeline: N≥25 per lane ≈ 5–6 months - slow lane; flag for patience in verdict scheduling.
- **Mark cadence:** 3-leg structure must be marked as a package (sum of leg mids), not leg-by-leg fills; standard platform poll cadence intraday is sufficient, EOD mark mandatory. The defensive trigger needs intraday underlying price (1-min), not intraday chain marks.
- **Multi-leg shape:** 4 lots / 3 strikes, ratio 1/−2/1, all puts, same expiry. After the defensive action (row 16) the residual is a 2-lot short put vertical - the ledger must support this shape mutation mid-trade or record it as close+reopen.
- **Capital-at-risk basis:** **width-credit basis** - CaR = (wide width − narrow width − net credit) × 100 per unit (row 20). This is the published max loss and the correct denominator for %-of-premium/%-of-CaR grading. Not debit-paid (there is no debit) and not a Reg-T proxy.
- **1-lot account-blind distortions:** minimal - the published form is inherently per-spread and defined-risk. Two distortions to note: (a) the published defensive roll "for a credit" may be unfillable at 1-lot shadow marks - the ledger's roll should only fire if the marked package credit is genuinely ≥ 0; (b) DDO's compounding narrative (500%/yr) relies on scaling contract count, which our 1-lot convention deliberately does not replicate - expected shadow P&L is per-unit only.
- **Consistency warning for the grader:** this strategy's exit rules (section 4) OVERRIDE platform exit doctrine - in particular there is NO stop loss and NO hold-to-expiry; the only exits are 50% PT, defensive close/roll, and the hard 21-DTE close.
- **Registered open question:** the only 45-DTE butterfly datapoint found (standard ATM debit fly, not this structure) was negative (−11.6%, 44% WR - §6). If the shadow ledger reproduces underperformance at 45 DTE, the published 21-DTE put-BWB variant (DDO spec, deltas 32/28/20, 2:1 wings, 10%-width credit floor, buy back at 2% of narrow width around 7 DTE) is the documented fallback candidate - do not silently migrate; re-register as a separate lane.
